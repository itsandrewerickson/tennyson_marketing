# Target Buyer Avatar: Tennyson Street Property

## Primary Avatar: The Small Multifamily Developer

### One-Sentence Summary
A hands-on real estate investor or small development company looking for entitled projects in premium San Diego neighborhoods where they can build, stabilize, and either hold for cash flow or sell at a profit.

### Profile
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Company Size** | Solo operator to 5-person shop |
| **Experience** | Built 2-10 small multifamily projects |
| **Capital** | $500K-$2M liquid for down payment + construction |
| **Geography** | San Diego County focused |
| **Typical Project** | 4-12 unit buildings, ADU conversions, small lot subdivisions |

### What They're Looking For
1. **Entitled deals** - Plans already approved, reduce timeline risk
2. **Premium locations** - Point Loma, North Park, Hillcrest, La Jolla
3. **Clear path to profit** - Obvious exit strategy
4. **Manageable scale** - Not too big, not too small
5. **Regulatory moats** - Something competitors can't easily replicate

### Pain Points
1. Finding land in San Diego is brutally competitive
2. Entitlement process is slow and expensive ($50K-$100K+ in soft costs)
3. New regulations constantly changing what's buildable
4. Hard to find deals that pencil at today's prices
5. Brokers bring them picked-over deals after institutions pass

### Why This Deal Appeals to Them
- **Skip the entitlement phase** - Plans approved, ready to build
- **Grandfathered rights** - Literally impossible to replicate this deal
- **Premium neighborhood** - Point Loma commands top rents
- **Multiple exit strategies** - Build & hold, build & sell units, sell plans to another developer
- **Story to tell LPs/lenders** - Clear narrative for raising capital

## Secondary Avatar: The Ambitious Owner-Occupier

### One-Sentence Summary
A real estate-savvy professional who wants to live in Point Loma while building wealth through ADU development - the ultimate house hack.

### Profile
| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | 30-45 |
| **Income** | $200K+ household |
| **Real Estate Experience** | Owns 1-3 properties, familiar with ADUs |
| **Motivation** | Live in dream neighborhood while building equity |
| **Risk Tolerance** | Medium-high, willing to manage construction |

### What They're Looking For
1. **Move-in ready main house** - Don't want to live in a construction zone initially
2. **Development upside** - Build ADUs over time for rental income
3. **Location they love** - Would live in Point Loma regardless
4. **Financing flexibility** - Can use owner-occupied loan products

### Pain Points
1. Point Loma prices are out of reach for pure consumption purchase
2. Need the rental income story to justify the price
3. Want development potential but scared of permitting complexity
4. Hard to find properties with both livability and upside

### Why This Deal Appeals to Them
- **Renovated main house** - Move in immediately
- **Plans done** - Just need to build, not figure out what to build
- **8 future income streams** - Massive cash flow potential
- **Brag-worthy story** - "I bought a house with 8 ADUs in Point Loma"

## Who We Are NOT Targeting

### Institutional Investors
- Too small for their minimum check size
- Can't compete on price with local operators
- Slower decision-making process

### Pure Flippers
- This is a development play, not a cosmetic flip
- Requires construction expertise and capital

### Passive Investors
- This requires active management of construction
- Not a turnkey rental property

### Out-of-State Buyers
- Local knowledge critical for San Diego development
- Harder to manage construction remotely

---

## Messaging Implications

### Lead With
1. **Grandfathered rights** - The regulatory moat
2. **Plans approved** - Skip the hardest part
3. **Point Loma** - The location sells itself
4. **Multiple exit strategies** - Flexibility reduces risk

### Acknowledge
1. Construction required - This is a development project
2. Deed restrictions on 3 units - Part of the approval
3. Limited parking - Realistic about constraints

### Avoid
1. Guaranteeing returns - All projections are estimates
2. Overselling simplicity - Building 8 ADUs is real work
3. High-pressure tactics - Qualified buyers will recognize value
